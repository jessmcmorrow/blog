A Vegan poem I wrote: Small children play In the park near my house. They throw rocks at each other They hunt out a mouse. But the mouse that they find Is actually a rat. It's huge and it's hungry, It's as big as a cat!

They try to dehunt the rat But they cannot escape, "This was all meant to be fun! A bit of a jape!", Is what one podgy child shouts, with a mouth full of fear. He pictures the rat gnawing On his cheek and his ear. Will his mum still love him If the rat chews him in half? Or will she drown poor Peter, Perhaps in the bath.

His friend is called Meg, And she's terrified too. She once got bit by a rat In a small petting zoo. She hazards a guess It could happen once more, So she legs it all The way to her front door.

But what Peter and Meg Both fail to address, Is they regularly eat rat, And not under duress. They eat it in wraps, And in nugget form - It's in every pack lunch, That appears in the dorm. They stupidly assume that all meat is meat, When sometimes it's rat Or sometimes it's feet.

So next time you're out playing, Try to catch a rat, Because they're full of good protein And the meat is low fat.
